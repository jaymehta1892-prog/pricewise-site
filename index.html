The part most guides skip

A store not price matching doesn't make its flyer useless. There are two different roles a store can play, and mixing them up is where most confusion comes from.

Source flyer vs. matching store

A matching store is where you check out and get the lower price applied. A source flyer is the ad you show to prove the lower price exists. Walmart and Food Basics won't match anything — but their flyers are accepted as source flyers at the stores that will. So a Food Basics deal is still worth having; you just take it to FreshCo or No Frills instead of to Food Basics.

This is also why "who has the cheapest flyer this week" is the wrong question. The useful question is: of the things already on my list, which ones are matchable somewhere near me?

The fine print that gets matches rejected

Every one of these programs is run at the store's discretion, and policies vary between locations of the same banner. These are the rules that trip people up most often across all four:

What kills a match

  • Size mismatch. The #1 rejection. The flyer says 500g, your pack is 375g — denied. Brand, size and attributes have to be identical.
  • Missing dates. If your screenshot doesn't show the flyer's valid dates, it won't be honoured. Capture the whole ad, not just the price.
  • Out-of-area competitor. Each store defines its own "geographic trade area" and keeps a list of the competitors it will match. That list is usually posted near the entrance — it's often not on the website.
  • Online-only pricing. E-commerce and "online exclusive" prices are excluded almost everywhere. The competitor needs a physical store nearby.
  • Promo pricing. "Spend $X get $Y", BOGO, "free" offers, clearance, and loyalty-program or member-only prices are excluded.
  • Self-checkout. Matches are applied by a cashier before the item is scanned. Use a staffed lane.

What's usually fine

  • Up to 4 units of the same item, per customer, per transaction.
  • Digital flyers. You don't need paper — showing the ad on your phone is standard.
  • Store-brand comparisons at FreshCo, which explicitly allows matching a competitor's private label against its own.

How to actually get the match

Order matters here — a few of these only work if you've done them before you're standing at the till.

  1. Check your list against this week's flyers before you leave. Matching is only worth the effort on the items you were buying anyway.
  2. Screenshot the full ad — product, size, price and valid dates in one frame.
  3. Confirm the competitor is on that store's list. Look for the posted competitor board near the entrance.
  4. Go to a staffed lane, not self-checkout or express.
  5. Show the ad before the item is scanned. Say you'd like to price match, and hand over your phone.
  6. If a cashier is unsure, politely ask for a manager. Policy application varies person to person more than it varies store to store.

Common questions

Does Walmart price match in Canada?

No. Walmart Canada discontinued its Ad Match program on October 15, 2020, and moved to an everyday-low-price strategy instead. It will still honour its own Walmart.ca pricing in store, but it will not match a competitor's flyer.

Do Metro or Food Basics price match?

Neither one matches competitor flyer prices. The Food Basics flyer is still worth watching, though — it's accepted as a source flyer at the chains that do match, so you can bring its deals to FreshCo or No Frills.

How many items can I price match at once?

The standard cap across the Ontario programs is 4 units of the same identical item, per customer, per transaction. There's generally no cap on how many different items you match — though matching thirty items in one trip is a good way to test a cashier's patience.

Can I price match at self-checkout?

Generally no. The match has to be applied by a cashier before the item is scanned, so use a staffed lane. Express lanes often won't do it either.

Can I stack a coupon on top of a price match?

Manufacturer coupons can often be applied on top of a matched price in Ontario, but this is applied at the store's discretion and isn't guaranteed. Store-specific promotional discounts generally can't be combined with a match.

Do all locations of the same chain follow the same policy?

No — and this is the single most frustrating part of price matching. No Frills stores are franchises, and each one sets its own competitor list and trade area. Two No Frills a few kilometres apart can genuinely give you different answers. Always check the competitor board at the store you're actually shopping at.
